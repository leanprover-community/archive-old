Horatiu Cheval (Sep 05 2021 at 20:37):

As part of a larger project, I implemented a (very basic for now) theory of so-called WWW-hyperbolic spaces, which are essentially metric spaces (X,d)(X, d)(X,d) endowed with a mapping W:[0,1]×X×X→XW : [0, 1] \times X \times X \to XW:[0,1]×X×X→X, satisfying certain axioms. Here, W(α,x,y)W(\alpha, x, y)W(α,x,y) is intended to be interpreted as an "abstract convex combination" of parameter α\alphaα between the points xxx and yyy. Examples are of course vector spaces, but also structures from geodesic geometry, like CAT(0)CAT(0)CAT(0) spaces.

I am wondering whether such constructions would have a place in mathlib.
