This came up in my Group Theory course. There was this yucky definition of "block"s of a group action. Let GGG be a group acting on a set Ω\OmegaΩ. A non-empty subset BBB of Ω\OmegaΩ is said to be a block if for each g∈Gg \in Gg∈G, either gB=BgB=BgB=B or gB∩B=∅gB \cap B = \emptysetgB∩B=∅. Everything became much easier when I realised a block is just an equivalence class for an equivalence relation with the property a∼b  ⟹  ∀g,ga∼gba \sim b \implies \forall g, ga \sim gba∼b⟹∀g,ga∼gb, or equivalently the preimage of a point under a morphism of GGG-sets.
