Adam Topaz (Nov 30 2022 at 22:45):

If you have the regular nnn-gon, centered in the plane, with a vertex on the yyy-axis, you can define rrr to be rotation counter-clockwise by 2⋅π/n2 \cdot \pi /n2⋅π/n radians (equiv. multiplication by exp⁡(2⋅π⋅i/n)\exp(2 \cdot \pi \cdot i / n)exp(2⋅π⋅i/n) in the complex plane. Also define sss to be reflection about the yyy-axis.

With these conventions, the collection of all symmetries of the regular nnn-gon are {r0,r1,…,rn−1,r0s,r1s,…,rn−1s}\{r^0,r^1,\ldots,r^{n-1},r^0s,r^1s,\ldots,r^{n-1}s\}{r0,r1,…,rn−1,r0s,r1s,…,rn−1s}. The multiplication rules follow from the formulas: rn=s2=(rs)2=1r^n = s^2 = (rs)^2 = 1rn=s2=(rs)2=1 (left as an exercise).
