Anatole Dedecker (Aug 07 2022 at 18:34):

Maybe it helps to think about what the statement looks like when applied to the whole space. In the topology library we like to make definitions about sets, but it is often harder to parse. Here, this really just means "a topological space XXX is compact iff each nontrivial filter on XXX has a cluster point" (docs#cluster_pt)

Anatole Dedecker (Aug 07 2022 at 18:52):

Here is a mathematical proof. We'll use the dual characterization of compactness (with intersections of closed sets), and the fact that cluster points of a filter F\mathcal{F}F are exactly the elements of ⋂A∈FAˉ\bigcap_{A\in\mathcal{F}}\bar{A}⋂A∈F​Aˉ.

  • First, assume XXX is compact, and let F\mathcal{F}F be a nontrivial filter on XXX. By contradiction, assume that F\mathcal{F}F has no cluster point, i.e ⋂A∈FAˉ=∅\bigcap_{A\in\mathcal{F}}\bar{A} = \varnothing⋂A∈F​Aˉ=∅. Since XXX is compact, that gives a finite set J⊆FJ \subseteq\mathcal{F}J⊆F with ⋂A∈JAˉ=∅\bigcap_{A\in J}\bar{A} = \varnothing⋂A∈J​Aˉ=∅ hence ∅=⋂A∈JA∈F\varnothing=\bigcap_{A\in J} A\in\mathcal{F}∅=⋂A∈J​A∈F so F\mathcal{F}F is trivial, contradiction.
  • Second, assume XXX is "filter-compact", and let (Ai)i∈I(A_i)_{i\in I}(Ai​)i∈I​ be a family of closed sets of XXX with ⋂i∈IAi=∅\bigcap_{i\in I} A_i = \varnothing⋂i∈I​Ai​=∅. By contradiction, assume that for all finite J⊆IJ\subseteq IJ⊆I, ⋂i∈JAi≠∅\bigcap_{i\in J} A_i \neq \varnothing⋂i∈J​Ai​=∅. Then the filter F\mathcal{F}F generated by {Ai}i∈I\{A_i\}_{i\in I}{Ai​}i∈I​ is nontrivial, which gives that ∅≠⋂A∈FAˉ⊆⋂i∈IAiˉ=⋂i∈IAi=∅\varnothing\ne\bigcap_{A\in\mathcal{F}}\bar{A}\subseteq\bigcap_{i\in I}\bar{A_i}=\bigcap_{i\in I} A_i = \varnothing∅=⋂A∈F​Aˉ⊆⋂i∈I​Ai​ˉ​=⋂i∈I​Ai​=∅, which is a contradiction
