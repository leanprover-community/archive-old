Yury G. Kudryashov (Jun 29 2022 at 11:29):

I suggest that we reformulate (some? most?) separation axioms in terms of filters. E.g.,

  • T0T_0T0​ space: inseparable x y implies x = y, see #15046;
  • T1T_1T1​ space: leave as is; the equivalent statement "specializes x y implies x = y" is less natural;
  • T2T_2T2​ space: if x ≠ y, then their nhds are disjoint;
  • T2.5T_{2.5}T2.5​ space: if x ≠ y, then "closures" of their nhds are disjoint (needs a new definition filter.map_sets with filter.comap = map_sets preimage)
  • regular space: if s is a closed set and x does not belong to this set, then nhds x and set_nhds s are disjoint;
  • normal space: if s and t are disjoint closed sets, then set_nhds s and set_nhds t are disjoint.

What do you think?
