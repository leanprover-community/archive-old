@Yury G. Kudryashov , we discussed adding an axiom to normed_ring, that the norm of 1 be 1. At the time we agreed to leave it, but I would like to propose making the change now, because I have a use in mind.

The use: I would like to show that in a complete normed ring, ∥(x + t)⁻¹∥ is O(1) as t → 0. The argument is to bound ∥(x + t)⁻¹∥ by
∥x−1∑i=0∞(tx−1)i∥≤∥x−1∥(∥1∥+∑i=1∞∥tx−1∥i)\lVert x^{-1}\sum_{i=0}^\infty(tx^{-1})^i\rVert\leq \lVert x^{-1}\rVert \left(\lVert 1\rVert + \sum_{i=1}^\infty\lVert tx^{-1}\rVert ^i\right)∥x−1∑i=0∞​(tx−1)i∥≤∥x−1∥(∥1∥+∑i=1∞​∥tx−1∥i).

If ∥1∥=1\lVert 1\rVert=1∥1∥=1 this can be written crisply as ∥x−1∥(1−∥tx−1∥)−1\lVert x^{-1}\rVert \left(1 - \lVert tx^{-1}\rVert \right)^{-1}∥x−1∥(1−∥tx−1∥)−1. It's obviously still possible without the axiom, just a little messier, so this only justifies adding the axiom if it is a good axiom for other reasons.

I have a counterexample to the axiom. The trace-class norm of a finite rank operator is the minimum of ∑∥ai∥∥bi∗∥\sum \|a_i\| \|b_i^*\|∑∥ai​∥∥bi∗​∥ over all representations of the operator as ∑ai⊗bi∗\sum a_i \otimes b_i^*∑ai​⊗bi∗​ where aia_iai​ is an element of the space and bi∗b_i^*bi∗​ an element of the dual.
