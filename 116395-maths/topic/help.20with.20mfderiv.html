However, it is not at all obvious that the Lean implementation really respects this canonical decomposition (and if it does it is more a coincidence that a deliberate design choice).

So, I have a question: are you sure this is a statement you want to prove and to use?

Sebastien Gouezel (Sep 26 2020 at 14:56):

You say "access the local coordinates on TTMTTMTTM induced by MMM", but the difficulty is that there are several such coordinates. So, a good definition should be independent of the choice of such coordinates. For instance, your definition

/-- The `join` operation of the tangent bundle monad.
    ‚ü®x,x',v,v'‚ü© ‚Ü¶ ‚ü®x,x'‚ü© and ‚ü®x,x',v,v'‚ü© ‚Ü¶ ‚ü®x,v‚ü© satisfies one of the two unit laws. Their sum satisfies both.  -/

Œª ‚ü®‚ü®x, x'‚ü©, v, v'‚ü©, ‚ü®x, (v + x')‚ü©

is not obviously coordinate-independent. A very good practice in differential geometry is to try to recast things in canonical terms (just using the projection from the tangent bundle to the manifold, and its derivative). Here, you have a point ppp in the tangent bundle (that you denote with (x,x‚Ä≤)(x, x')(x,x‚Ä≤) -- this is a little bit misleading as x‚Ä≤x'x‚Ä≤ is a tangent vector), and then you have a tangent vector to this point, that you denote with (v,v‚Ä≤)(v, v')(v,v‚Ä≤) but here you are abusing coordinates. Instead, let's just denote it with WWW. Then, you have the map proj:TM‚ÜíMproj : TM \to Mproj:TM‚ÜíM. You can consider its differential at ppp, which is a map Tpproj:Tp(TM)‚ÜíTxMT_p proj : T_p (TM) \to T_x MTp‚Äãproj:Tp‚Äã(TM)‚ÜíTx‚ÄãM. You can take the image of WWW under this map, and you get canonically a well defined vector in TxMT_x MTx‚ÄãM. If I understand correctly, your map is therefore (p,W)‚Ü¶(x,(v+Tpproj(W)))(p, W) \mapsto (x, (v + T_p proj (W)))(p,W)‚Ü¶(x,(v+Tp‚Äãproj(W))) where p=(x,v)p = (x, v)p=(x,v). If you try to work with this expression, things should go much more smoothly.
