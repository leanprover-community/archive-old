Kevin Buzzard (Mar 08 2022 at 22:13):

My favourite proof in there is the proof that the integers of Q(sqrt(-19)) is not a Euclidean domain (but it is a PID); it's surprisingly short (and works for 43,67,163 as well). This shows that you need infinitely many units for this PID<->ED thing to be possibly true. But the moment you have units, arguments such as the Q(sqrt(-19)) one break down.
