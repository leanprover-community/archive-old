This seems like a job for Lagrange multipliers and Jacobi's formula. A sketch:

First, consider the submanifold of k×kk\times kk×k matrices where row iii has length cic_ici​, with c1,…,ck>0c_1,\dots,c_k>0c1​,…,ck​>0. Let AiA_iAi​ denote the matrix AAA, but setting all but row iii to 000. The matrices A1,…,AkA_1,\dots,A_kA1​,…,Ak​ span the subspace of the tangent bundle that are orthogonal to the submanifold at AAA.

One version of Jacobi's formula is that ∂det⁡(A)/∂Aij=adj⁡(A)ji\partial\det(A)/\partial{A_{ij}} = \operatorname{adj}(A)_{ji}∂det(A)/∂Aij​=adj(A)ji​, where AijA_{ij}Aij​ is entry (i,j)(i,j)(i,j) of AAA. Assuming det⁡(A)≠0\det(A)\neq 0det(A)=0, then AAA is invertible, so adj⁡(A)=det⁡(A)A−1\operatorname{adj}(A)=\det(A)A^{-1}adj(A)=det(A)A−1. David Wärn (Oct 20 2021 at 21:42):

You can rephrase my argument above to make it more direct: if AAA is a square matrix, then you should be able to show that there exists a unitary matrix UUU such that AUAUAU is upper triangular. This has the same (absolute value of) determinant as AAA, and the norms of the rows are the same. But now the conclusion is clear, since the determinant is a product of diagonal entries, and the norms are lower bounded by diagonal entries.
