Michael Stoll (Aug 31 2022 at 16:12):

In #16290, I introduce a few lemmas

lemma odd.mod_even_iff {n a : ℕ} (ha : even a) : odd n ↔ odd (n % a)
lemma even.mod_even_iff {n a : ℕ} (ha : even a) : even n ↔ even (n % a)
lemma odd.mod_even {n a : ℕ} (hn : odd n) (ha : even a) : odd (n % a)
lemma even.mod_even {n a : ℕ} (hn : even n) (ha : even a) : even (n % a)
lemma odd.factors_ne_two {n p : ℕ} (hn : odd n) (hp : p ∈ n.factors) : p ≠ 2

(in part based on suggestions by @Junyan Xu).
Question: Should they full name be nat.odd.... or just odd....?
The first seems more appropriate in view of the statements, but the second is more convenient for dot notation.
Opinions?
