Sebastien Gouezel (Jun 22 2020 at 18:35):

You can indeed give a proof using the fact that E →L[𝕜] E is a Banach algebra. If you need shortcuts, you can also give a pretty direct proof, by noting that if A is invertible, then you have an inverse for A + h for small enough h, given by the series ∑(−A−1h)nA−1\sum (-A^{-1} h)^n A^{-1}∑(−A−1h)nA−1 (where the convergence follows from the norm convergence, which follows from the convergence of geometric series -- and all this is already in mathlib).
