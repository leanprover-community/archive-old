Topic: `general_linear_group` versus `automorphism_group`

{% raw %}

Anne Baanen (May 20 2020 at 10:23):

@Patrick Massot pointed out that linear_algebra.basic contains two definitions of the group of invertible linear maps: the instance automorphism_group gives a group structure on linear_equiv R M M, and the type general_linear_group is defined as units (linear_map R M M). Is there a reason that we don't just define general_linear_group R M := linear_equiv R M M?

Chris Hughes (May 20 2020 at 11:18):

I don't think so, This is probably just different people PRing at different times without checking properly for duplication. I would go with the linear_equiv definition, and I'm not sure which name is better.
