I've been working on proving the existence of integral curves for smooth vector fields on manifolds. So far I've shown that the integral curve exists in an open interval around t = 0 whenever the initial point x : M is not on the boundary. Would anyone like to take a look?

Next steps would be the fundamental theorem of flows and maybe exponential maps of Lie algebra being integral curves of left-invariant vector fields on the Lie group.
