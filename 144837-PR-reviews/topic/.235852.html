@Sebastien Gouezel There is something very simple I'm misunderstanding on this PR, could you set me in the right direction? I would have thought that the formal left/right inverse of the function-defined-by-a-series f(x)=3+xf(x)=3 + xf(x)=3+x is the function g(y)=−3+yg(y)=-3+yg(y)=−3+y. However, your left/right inverses have zero constant term?

Sebastien Gouezel (Jan 27 2021 at 16:07):

Yes. I should probably insist more on that in the docstrings. If you take the constant term into account, then you need to put an infinite series in the inverse in general, so you run into convergence questions. This is already true for the composition of formal power series: if you use the constant term, then you have infinite series everywhere, so the composition is not associative or whatever if some series are converging and others are not. The only situation where everything is well behaved is when the constant terms are zero. So, at the informal level, I only define composition and inverse for series with zero constant coefficient. However, I give it as a totally defined function, just ignoring the constant coefficient if it is nonzero.
