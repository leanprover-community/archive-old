Kyle Miller (Jul 28 2022 at 16:16):

I've added the 100.yaml entry.

Euler's solution to the Konigsberg problem was to consider sequences of letters ABCAD... such that letters are consecutive iff the corresponding bodies of land are connected by a bridge and such that unordered pairs of consecutive letters appear the correct number of times. So perhaps the more Eulerian way to model it would be a simple graph for the underlying connectivity and then look for walks whose edges are some specific multiset, rather than using multigraphs.

I can't possibly speak for Euler, but I imagine if we had the opportunity to ask him his opinion he'd wonder why the argument in this PR doesn't resolve the Konigsberg bridges problem.
