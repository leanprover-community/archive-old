This PR had been languishing for a while, and it's now been slightly improved. It's a corollary to another theorem, but the proof is a lot of nothing (just universe wrangling).

It would be make sense to eventually change everything to be in terms of cofiltered limits rather than inverse limits, and I think that will clean up the incidental complexity. The main issue seems to be that having the theorems take a directed system as a functor from a directed_order makes things less flexible -- the functor wants a specific category structure, rather than, say, the one from ulift. (I just don't have time right now to do this.)

This is being used in #7825 to take a statement that's true for fintypes and boost it to work for all types. The same sort of technique can be applied to, for example, boost a proof that all finite planar graphs are 4-colorable to a proof that all planar graphs are 4-colorable.
