Scott Morrison (Sep 19 2019 at 06:28):

Hi @Yury G. Kudryashov, regarding the desirability or otherwise of "explicit proofs", and @Johan Commelin complaining that some things previously done by tidy no longer were:

I think there's nothing necessarily wrong with replacing an "automated proof" with an "explicit proof", and indeed if the automation is running painfully slowly, it's a good thing to do. However, I find that
1. checking that all the easy proofs _can_ be done by automation is an excellent test of whether I have all my simp lemmas, coercions, instances, and ext lemmas set up correctly! tidy failing on something dumb often reveals a gap
2. trying to automate away "more proofs than really necessary" is an excellent driver for improving the automation!
3. finally, reading developments where all the "boring"/"easy" proofs are entirely invisible, because of automation, is much easier --- I only have to pay attention to the interesting bits, rather than having to identify the 90% of the text which is too boring to bother understanding.
