Heather Macbeth (Aug 05 2022 at 03:03):

@Winston Yin You can look at docs#circle.lie_group for a model. So, e.g., to show that inv : RÀ£ ‚Üí RÀ£ is m-smooth

  1. by cont_mdiff.of_comp_open_embedding it suffices to show that coe ‚àò inv : RÀ£ ‚Üí R is m-smooth
  2. the map coe ‚àò inv : RÀ£ ‚Üí R is equal to ring.inverse ‚àò coe (here docs#ring.inverse is this extended-by-junk-values "inverse")
  3. by cont_mdiff_open_embedding the map coe : RÀ£ ‚Üí R is m-smooth
  4. by docs#cont_diff_at_ring_inverse the map ring.inverse : R ‚Üí R is smooth (on the needed domain) and therefore m-smooth
  5. composition of m-smooth maps is m-smooth
