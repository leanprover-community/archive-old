Tobias Schmude (Jun 28 2022 at 18:45):

Junyan Xu said:

Unfortuantely I'm not a category theory expert and my motivation mainly comes from algebraic geometry; I'm not sure what Prof stands for?

By Prof\mathrm{Prof}Prof I mean the bicategory of profunctors: 0-cells are small categories, 1-cells C→C′\mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{C}'C→C′ are profunctors, i.e. functors C′op×C→Set\mathcal{C}'^\mathrm{op} \times \mathcal{C} \to \mathrm{Set}C′op×C→Set, and 2-cells are natural transformations.

An arbitrary functor C→B\mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{B}C→B does not induce functors between its fibers, only profunctors. That's why they are relevant here.
