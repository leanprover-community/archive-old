Anne Baanen (Aug 21 2020 at 10:14):

decidable_eq Œπ means that given any two i j : Œπ, there is an algorithm for deciding whether i = j or not. This makes it possible to write out the identity matrix indexed by Œπ: 1 i j = if i = j then 1 else 0, for example in a tactic that solves a problem by computing with matrices.

Anne Baanen (Aug 21 2020 at 10:16):

Kevin Buzzard (Aug 21 2020 at 12:40):

@Nicol√≤ Cavalleri Lean makes a random choice of basis, as we do, when making the definition. The definition involves the choice. The theorem that it's independent of the choice is a different object in Lean. Lean's version of the axiom of choice is super-strong. It chooses once and for all, at the beginning of time, an element from each nonempty type, and then we never change those choices.

Reid Barton (Aug 21 2020 at 12:46):

The difference is in math we don't consider something like this to be a definition until we've checked that it is independent of the choices involved.

Reid Barton (Aug 21 2020 at 12:46):

That's how we know it is a sensible notion that behaves as expected under isomorphisms, etc.
