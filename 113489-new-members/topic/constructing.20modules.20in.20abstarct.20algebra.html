Kyle Miller (Jul 10 2021 at 22:40):

Maybe this is a reason to believe that modules over semirings should be commutative monoids. More generally, a monoid object in an appropriate kind of category (a monoidal category to be precise) is an object AAA along with morphisms μ:A×A→A\mu:A\times A\to Aμ:A×A→A and η:1→A\eta:1\to Aη:1→A, where all the diagrams associated to the axioms of a monoid commute. A left module MMM over the monoid is another object along with a morphism α:A×M→M\alpha:A\times M\to Mα:A×M→M such that the diagrams associated to the axioms of a monoid action commute. A semiring is a monoid object in the category of commutative monoids, so a module is defined to be a commutative monoid.

One observation is that since you'd want something like (a+b)m=am+bm(a+b)m = am+bm(a+b)m=am+bm out of a module, and a+b=b+aa+b=b+aa+b=b+a is true for the semiring, then you'd have am+bm=bm+amam+bm=bm+amam+bm=bm+am. You may as well require m+n=n+mm+n=n+mm+n=n+m for all mmm and nnn in MMM.

Kyle Miller (Jul 10 2021 at 22:41):

From this point of view, modules are additive (rather than multiplicative) monoids because semirings are additive monoids (with additional multiplicative structure from μ\muμ).

David Wärn (Jul 11 2021 at 08:39):

Indeed for any module over any semiring, left- and right- distributivity means (m+n)+(m+n)=(1+1)(m+n)=(m+m)+(n+n)(m+n) + (m+n) = (1+1)(m+n) = (m+m) + (n+n)(m+n)+(m+n)=(1+1)(m+n)=(m+m)+(n+n). Assuming addition in the module is cancellative and associative we get n+m=m+nn+m = m+nn+m=m+n, so commutativity is automatic (same argument works for addition in semirings). Still, Lean could conceivable only require [add_group M] to say module R M, and prove commutativity from this.

Adam Topaz (Jul 12 2021 at 14:22):

Let me reiterate what Kyle said above. A (semi)ring is a monoid object in a specific monoidal category, and a module over a (semi)ring is an object with an action of said monoid object.

If you work with the category of sets, you get the usual notion of a monoid acting on a set.

If you work with the category Ab of abelian groups, you recover the usual notion of a module over a ring.
