Kyle Miller (Nov 30 2021 at 19:46):

I assume you mean where you take the quotient of GL(V) by the stabilizer of a subspace? Or maybe the quotient of n×kn\times kn×k matrices of rank kkk by the GL(k) action?

One thing I think would be nice (maybe not as the definition, but at least to show it's possible in Lean) would be to give the Grassmannian of kkk-dimensional subspaces its smooth topology in a way that doesn't depend on having to choose a subspace or a basis. Until recently, I thought you needed to choose an inner product for graph charts, but the following works (with a caveat): you can create a (non-maximal) atlas indexed by short exact sequences 1→U→V→W→11 \to U \to V \to W \to 1 1→U→V→W→1 that come with a section s:W→Vs:W\to Vs:W→V, where UUU is a kkk-dimensional subspace of VVV. Given sss, you can define an injective map hom⁡(U,W)→Grk(V)\hom(U, W) \to \mathrm{Gr}_k(V)hom(U,W)→Grk​(V) by f↦{u+sfu∣u∈U}f\mapsto \{u+sfu\mid u\in U\}f↦{u+sfu∣u∈U}.

The caveat is that we need to identify hom⁡(U,W)\hom(U, W)hom(U,W) with Euclidean space for all the charts to use the same model, so what also goes into indexing the charts would be a linear isomorphism hom⁡(U,W)→Rk(dim⁡V−k)\hom(U,W)\to \mathbb{R}^{k(\dim V-k)}hom(U,W)→Rk(dimV−k) in addition to the short exact sequence and the section.

Sebastien Gouezel (Dec 07 2021 at 08:21):

Most naive statements are not true for manifolds with boundary. The basic example to keep in mind is the following. Consider the graph MMM of the function x↦e−1/x2sin⁡(1/x)x \mapsto e^{-1/x^2} \sin (1/x)x↦e−1/x2sin(1/x). It is a smooth submanifold of R2\R^2R2. Now consider the inclusion iii of the upper half plane (with boundary) into R2\R^2R2. Then its differential is everywhere invertible, but still i−1(M)i^{-1}(M)i−1(M) is extremely nasty around 000 (it has infinitely many connected components, for instance).
