Here's a standalone bit that needs to be done: https://leanprover-community.github.io/liquid/sect0010.html#normed-to-cond
Every normed group VVV is a topological group, hence condensed: V(S)V(S)V(S) is the collection of continuous maps S→VS → VS→V.
But there is another description of this condensed abelian group: V(S)V(S)V(S) is also the completion of the group of locally constant maps S→VS → VS→V, for the sup-norm.
We need to know that the two points of view are equivalent.
