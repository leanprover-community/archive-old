The last line of the proof of https://leanprover-community.github.io/liquid/sect0008.html#Qprime-prop is

In general, one can pick a two-term free resolution of AAA and use the long exact sequence.

I am now wondering what the smoothest way is to do this in Lean.
The context is the following: Let AAA be an abelian group and 0→F₁→F₀→A→00 → F₁ → F₀ → A → 00→F₁→F₀→A→0 a free resolution. Let MMM be a condensed abelian group (which we can assume to be torsion-free). We want to show that Exti(M⊗A,N)\text{Ext}^i(M ⊗ A, N)Exti(M⊗A,N) vanishes, and we know already that Exti(M⊗Fj,N)\text{Ext}^i(M ⊗ F_j, N)Exti(M⊗Fj​,N) vanishes for free FjF_jFj​.

I think the easiest way to continue is to show that 0→M⊗F₁→M⊗F₀→M⊗A→00 → M ⊗ F₁ → M ⊗ F₀ → M ⊗ A → 00→M⊗F₁→M⊗F₀→M⊗A→0 is short exact, using the fact that MMM is torsion-free. What I really want to avoid is any theoretical properties of Tor, because we don't have anything about it. What is the cheapest (in terms of ingredients) way to prove (on paper) that a torsion-free abelian group is flat?
Note that we don't even need to show that M ⊗ _ is exact: a direct proof that it sends injective maps between free abelian groups to injective maps is all we need.
