Johan Commelin (Oct 11 2021 at 13:25):

I think I've worked out a full proof for the following fact (after some help by Peter :thank_you:). Let MMM be a condensed abelian group that is torsion free

(∀i,Exti(Q′(M),V)=0)  ⟹  Exti(M,V)=0\left(∀ i, \text{Ext}^i(Q'(M), V) = 0\right) \quad \implies \quad \text{Ext}^i(M, V) = 0(∀i,Exti(Q′(M),V)=0)⟹Exti(M,V)=0

The proof I have makes use of tensor products of condensed abelian groups, and the fact that the commute with colimits. So we probably want to prove a ⊗⊣Hom⊗ ⊣ \text{Hom}⊗⊣Hom adjunction.

Johan Commelin (Oct 11 2021 at 13:44):

For the first claim, we write MMM as a filtered colimit of fin.gen. free objects Z[S]ℤ[S]Z[S], where SSS runs over finite subsets of MMM. (This can be done, because MMM was assumed torsion free.)
From the definition of Q′(M)Q'(M)Q′(M) we find Q′(M)=colimSQ′(Z[S])Q'(M) = \text{colim}_S Q'(ℤ[S])Q′(M)=colimS​Q′(Z[S]). By the homotopy property of Q′Q'Q′, we know that it is additive (as functor to the derived category, interesting for Lean!!) and therefore we get =colimSQ′(Z)S = \text{colim}_S Q'(ℤ)^S=colimS​Q′(Z)S.

Johan Commelin (Oct 11 2021 at 13:45):

Johan Commelin (Oct 11 2021 at 13:49):

Finally, for the second claim above, we take a free resolution F•F^•F• of AAA. We can then compute

Extj(A⊗M,V)=Hj(Hom(F•⊗M,V))\text{Ext}^j(A ⊗ M, V) = H_j(\text{Hom}(F^• ⊗ M, V))Extj(A⊗M,V)=Hj​(Hom(F•⊗M,V))

and since the FkF^kFk are free, we can pull them out of the tensor and the hom, and turn the direct sums into products. The result vanishes by the ind.hyp.

  (∀ i, is_iso $ ((Ext' i).map f).app B - ((Ext' i).obj A).map g) ↔
  (∀ i, is_iso $
    ((Ext i).map ((BD.eval F).op.map f)).app ((single _ 0).obj B) -

Johan Commelin (May 31 2022 at 05:57):

The administration of working with

is_iso $
    ((Ext i).map ((BD.eval F).op.map f)).app ((single _ 0).obj B) -
    ((Ext i).obj ((BD.eval F).op.obj A)).map ((single _ 0).map g))

aka

f∗−g∗ ⁣:Exti(Q′(A),B)→Exti(Q′(A),B)is an isof^* - g_* \colon \text{Ext}^i(Q'(A), B) \to \text{Ext}^i(Q'(A), B) \qquad \text{is an iso}f∗−g∗​:Exti(Q′(A),B)→Exti(Q′(A),B)is an iso

for maps f ⁣:A→Af \colon A \to Af:A→A and g ⁣:B→Bg \colon B \to Bg:B→B didn't look very appetizing. So I decided to bundle everything up into the category of endomorphisms, with objects (A,f)(A,f)(A,f).
If AAA was an object of Ab\text{Ab}Ab, then the resulting category is of course equivalent to Z[X]-Mod\mathbb{Z}[X]\text{-Mod}Z[X]-Mod.

The upshot is that (modulo a few sorrys) we now only need to work with

is_zero (((Ext i).obj A').obj B')

where A' and B' are now objects of an arbitrary ab.cat (potentially this category of endomorphisms).
