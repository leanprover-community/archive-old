Topic: ✔ Golfing `cases` followed by `exact`

{% raw %}

Dylan MacKenzie (ecstatic-morse) (Feb 23 2022 at 23:55):

I often write tactic mode proofs that end in cases and exact, like so:

cases hx with a ha, -- hx: ∃ (a : ℤ) ...
exact ⟨a, le_trans (le_of_lt $ sub_one_lt x) ha⟩ -- ⊢ ∃ (a : ℤ) ...

What's the equivalent in term-mode? Is there a way to combine the two lines?

Eric Wieser (Feb 23 2022 at 23:55):

It depends what the type of hx is

Eric Wieser (Feb 23 2022 at 23:56):

refine h.imp _ might work there

Dylan MacKenzie (ecstatic-morse) (Feb 23 2022 at 23:56):

It's an existential.

Dylan MacKenzie (ecstatic-morse) (Feb 23 2022 at 23:56):

hx: ∃ (a : ℤ), x ≤ f a
⊢ ∃ (a : ℤ), x - 1 ≤ f a

Dylan MacKenzie (ecstatic-morse) (Feb 23 2022 at 23:56):

is the tactic state.

Dylan MacKenzie (ecstatic-morse) (Feb 24 2022 at 00:11):

Exists.imp was indeed what I wanted. Thanks @Eric Wieser!

Notification Bot (Feb 24 2022 at 00:11):

Dylan MacKenzie (ecstatic-morse) has marked this topic as resolved.

Eric Wieser (Feb 24 2022 at 00:11):

The general answer is of course that there's isn't always such a function

Eric Wieser (Feb 24 2022 at 00:12):

But you can write let ⟨a, ha⟩ := hx in ... to do cases in term mode.
