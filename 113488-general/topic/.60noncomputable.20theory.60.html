Eric Wieser (Oct 06 2020 at 08:29):

Is putting noncomputable theory at the top of the file different to putting noncomputable on every def?

Kyle Miller (Oct 06 2020 at 08:31):

I believe it's mostly equivalent, except that definitions that don't need noncomputable won't be marked noncomputable. Lean will warn if you put noncomputable in front of a definition where it's unnecessary.

Eric Wieser (Oct 06 2020 at 08:32):

Is one approach preferred over the other?

Scott Morrison (Oct 06 2020 at 10:17):

I typically use noncomputable theory on the principle that in most mathlib files we don't care about computability, and it's better to get it out of the way quickly.

Scott Morrison (Oct 06 2020 at 10:17):

I only use noncomputable on individual definitions if it is valuable as documentation, because a reader may care exactly which definitions are or are not computable.

Eric Wieser (Oct 06 2020 at 10:35):

I assume you're fine then with my change in #4471 which replaces noncomputable theory with two noncomputable keywords.
